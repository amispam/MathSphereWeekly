Gauss's Perspective on Algebraic Solutions

Gauss's views on the algebraic solution of an equation were evident from his 1801 thesis, where he remarked that such a solution was no more meaningful than inventing a symbol for the root and declaring the equation's root to be equal to that symbol.

Oystein Ore's Account of Niels Henrik Abel's Final Days

Abel's weakness and cough worsened, confining him to bed except for brief moments while it was made. Though he occasionally tried to work on mathematics, he was too weak to write. He often reflected on his poverty and Fru Hansteen's kindness, remaining kind and patient throughout. On the night of April 5, he endured severe agony but grew quieter by morning, passing away at eleven o'clock.
