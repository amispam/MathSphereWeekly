Johann Carl Friedrich Gauss [1777-1855]

Since \(17\) is a Fermat prime, the regular heptadecagon is a constructible polygon, meaning it can be constructed using only a compass and an unmarked straightedge. This remarkable result was demonstrated by Carl Friedrich Gauss in \(1796\) at the age of \(18\). His proof marked the first significant advancement in the construction of regular polygons in over \(2000\) years.

A fine description of Gauss by two of his students

He typically sat in a relaxed posture, slightly stooped, gazing downward with his hands folded above his lap. He spoke freely, clearly, and in a straightforward manner. However, when he wished to emphasize a new perspective, he would lift his head, turn to someone nearby, and fix them with his striking, penetrating blue eyes as he delivered his point.

Oystein Ore's Account of Niels Henrik Abel's Final Days
